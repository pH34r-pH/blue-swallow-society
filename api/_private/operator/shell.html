Manifesto

Operator surfaces are evidence rooms, not arcades.

I keep the cover clean and the operator layer exact. Public ritual stays public; field telemetry, source provenance, and paper state cross the authenticated seam only when they have earned it.

Street hardware leaves marks. Patched joints, oxidized metal, and old constraints stay visible because wear is history, not decoration. Executive restraint makes that history legible under pressure.

No glow without signal. No ornament without status. Every claim keeps its source, every control states its consequence, and every system remains useful when the mood lighting dies.

Modern usage notes

  • Choom, preem, nova, and gonk are the most legible modern carry-overs for everyday dialogue.
  • Cyberspace, ICE, cyberdeck, and jacking in read as genre shorthand now, not just period-specific jargon.
  • Simstim and console cowboy lean Sprawl-specific; use when the voice wants Gibson more than generic Night City noise.
  • Meatspace may read as internet-era slang before Gibson; still useful, but it shifts register.
  • Characters should not sound interchangeable: corpo, fixer, nomad, and decker slang should reveal status and region.
